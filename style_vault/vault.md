## Style Rules

### Hook rules
- **Reversal sentence preferred:** Give the reader's expected answer first, then immediately overturn it
- **Short assertion second:** Brief declaration, no explanation, forces the reader forward
- Avoid question hooks (they read as tutorial-like)

### Paragraph rhythm rules
- Single-sentence paragraphs are not mandatory — 2–4 sentences can group together if semantically coherent, with one blank line between blocks
- Maximum paragraph length: 3–4 sentences; beyond that, split
- Delete all transition sentences ("So I decided to...", "That's why I...")
- No `---` dividers — use blank lines to separate naturally

### Emotional density rules
- **Conclusion before explanation:** State the insight first, then (optionally) explain why
- **Feeling over operation:** Write what you felt, not what steps you took
- Let the reader feel you walked this path, without telling them how to walk it
- **Specific over abstract:** "operational judgment" over "judgment"; concrete detail over general claim
- **Don't expose company context:** Keep references general ("environments" not "our environment")

### Structure rules
- One layer per post — don't stack multiple insights
- Ending sentence must be a standalone takeable insight, its own paragraph, usually the shortest
- Generous blank lines — designed for vertical mobile reading

### Publishing format rules (LinkedIn actual layout)
- Remove `---` dividers, use blank lines to separate naturally
- List items use LinkedIn bullet `·`, not markdown `-`
- Shorter paragraphs than draft, more compact
- Delete transitional lead-ins — go straight to content

## Anti-patterns (things you're likely to delete)

<!-- Add to this list every time you edit a draft. These are your actual no-gos. -->

- "Here's what I learned:" — too formal, reads like an article not a conversation
- "I want to share..." — weak, lacks confidence
- Over-explaining sentences (more than 1 sentence of explanation = lecturing)
- "In conclusion" / "To summarize" type endings
- Operational step lists (unless the post is explicitly a methodology post)
- `---` dividers — use blank lines only
- Vague insider observations — replace with specific, perceptible detail lists
- Explaining the metaphor after using it — build it and walk away
- Supporting a conclusion with an example sentence right after — state the conclusion and stop
- em dash `—` → use en dash `–` instead (more conversational rhythm)
- Adjective emphasis — remove surprisingly, clearly, obviously etc.
